INTRODUCTION

Fewer people are politically active while society is becoming more complex, so it is important to have a safe way to gather knowledge and information. Mathijs van Buesekom noted that the current GroenLinks platform is closed and not accessible to regular members, which reduces transparency.In this project,

I am designing a safe process that is partly public and partly closed for inviting internal members and external experts.

Based on research by Martijn Bosman, there is a need for a safe, transparent, and democratic process that allows members and citizens to participate and contribute to political discussions.

ABOUT CLIENTüï¥Ô∏è

Martijn Bosman is a lecturer at the Amsterdam University of Applied Sciences and a service designer. He is a member of GroenLinks and has researched the internal decision-making processes of political parties. His research showed a lack of participation and communication. Martijn aims to make political parties more democratic and transparent, and therefore proposes designing a safe digital interactive product to invite citizens and experts to participate in discussions within working groups.

PROBLEM

The current GroenLinks platform is closed, meaning regular members and citizens cannot access key information or discussions, which limits participation and transparency. This creates a gap between the party and its supporters. Martijn Bosman argues that a more transparent and democratic process is needed. To address this, an interactive digital product is needed that safely invites members and experts to participate in working groups.

0.1 RESEARCH

User Interviews (PERSONA)

To better understand the needs and pain points of potential users, I conducted interviews with chairmen of working groups from different sectors who fit the target audience profile. The interviews focused on their experiences with political platforms, their motivations for participation, and their concerns regarding security and transparency.Based on the insights gathered, I created a persona for a typical user:The persona is based on the interviews I conducted with chairpersons. Their main need is to actively involve both members and people outside the party

Stakeholder map

To identify all relevant parties involved in the invite process, I created a stakeholder map. This map helped visualize the relationships and interests of each stakeholder, including party members, external experts, citizens, and platform administrators. Understanding these dynamics was crucial for designing an inclusive and secure invite system that addresses the needs of all parties involved.

Requirements Specification

Based on the research findings, I developed a set of requirements for the invite system. These requirements focused on key aspects such as security measures, user experience, transparency features, and inclusivity considerations. The specification served as a guiding document throughout the design process, ensuring that the final product met the identified needs and addressed the challenges highlighted during the research phase.

0.2 IDEATION

Based on the research insights, I brainstormed various ideas for the invite system. I focused on creating a user-friendly interface that balances security with ease of use. I explored different methods for verifying identities, such as two-factor authentication and secure invitation links. Additionally, I considered features that would enhance transparency, such as activity logs and clear communication channels between members and invitees. After evaluating the ideas against the requirements specification, I selected the most promising concepts to develop further.

HKJ (How can you method)

By using the HKJ method, I explored how to improve the invite system's usability and security. This involved asking "How can you..." questions to identify potential solutions and innovations within the design process.

0.3 CONCEPT

INVITEFLOW

The process consists of multiple phases that invitees go through in order to participate in a working group. Throughout these steps, different privacy levels‚Äîpublic, open, private, and secret‚Äîare taken into account. This ensures that invitees only gain access to information relevant to their role, while safeguarding the confidentiality of the content. As a result, the right people receive the right information based on their access level.

ACCESS LEVEL

I created an access level overview with the help of cyber security experts and also by interviewing the chairpersons of the groenlinks political party for feedback.Invitees are granted access based on their role within the working group. The system supports four privacy levels: public, open, private, and secret. Public content is accessible to all users, while open content is visible to members of the working group. Private content is restricted to specific individuals or roles within the group, and secret content is only accessible to administrators or designated members.

  • Public: Accessible to all users.
  • Open: Visible to members of the working group.
  • Private: Restricted to specific individuals or roles within the group.
  • Secret: Only accessible to administrators or designated members.

VALIDATION

Usability Testing

To validate the effectiveness of the invite system, I conducted user testing sessions with potential users, including party members and external experts. Participants were asked to complete specific tasks within the system, such as accepting an invitation, verifying their identity, and accessing working group content. Feedback was collected on usability, security features, and overall user experience. Based on the feedback, I made necessary adjustments to improve the design and functionality of the invite system.

Requirements Completion

I was able to confirm that all requirements from the specification were met through the design and validation process. The invite system successfully addressed key aspects such as security measures, user experience, transparency features, and inclusivity considerations. Feedback from user testing indicated that the system was intuitive and easy to use, while also providing the necessary security and transparency features. Overall, the project met its objectives and delivered a secure and inclusive invite system for the political collaboration platform.

REFLECTION

Societal Impact

The invite system has the potential to significantly enhance political participation and transparency within the GroenLinks party. By providing a secure and inclusive platform for inviting members, experts, and citizens to participate in working groups, the system fosters greater engagement and collaboration. This can lead to more diverse perspectives being represented in political discussions, ultimately contributing to more democratic decision-making processes. Additionally, the emphasis on security and transparency helps build trust among users, encouraging more individuals to get involved in political activities.

Lessons learned

  • I have learnt to proactively seek feedback from stakeholders and users throughout the design process to ensure that the final product meets their needs and expectations.
  • I have learnt to not be afraid to fail and to embrace iterative design, using failures as opportunities for learning and improvement.
  • I have learnt to engage stakeholders early and often to ensure their needs and concerns are addressed in the design process.
  • I have learnt to master the courage to make design decisions based on research and user feedback, even when they challenge assumptions or conventions.
