INTRODUCTION

This project focuses on visualising complex data that is often difficult to interpret. The goal is to create clear and engaging visual representations of the data, making it easier for users to understand and derive insights from it. The data used in this project was collected from a group of students at the Amsterdam University of Applied Sciences. Based on the provided data, I designed a poster that visualises the key factors influencing the sleeping duration of this specific student group.

VARIABLES FOR EACH ANGLE

For each angle i was required to use more than 3 variables because using less than 3 variable makes the thinking process for coming up with the right chart easy and the goal was to challenge ourselves to think critically and creatively about how to represent complex data visually.

Angle 1: Does the travel of duration affect the sleeping time of students

This visualization shows semicircular arcs that all start from the same point. The radius of each arc represents the travel time in minutes, and the color shows how long the student slept. This means you can read two variables at the same time. The horizontal axis also relates to travel time, but the curved shape makes the length of each journey stand out more visually.

The variables i used for this angles are:

  • Place of residence
  • Sleeping time
  • Wake up time
Angle 2: The screen time and coffee consumption of the students

In this visualization, I explored the relationship between screen time, coffee consumption, and sleep duration among students. Each point in the scatterplot represents an individual student. The horizontal axis shows sleep duration in hours, while the vertical axis represents daily screen time in minutes. To support comparative reading, I used shape to indicate gender (female, male, or unknown) and color to encode the number of coffees consumed per day. This allows viewers to identify clusters and patterns without overwhelming them with visual complexity. From a UX perspective, the goal of this design was to balance readability with exploration. The legend is positioned prominently to guide interpretation, and the visual encoding choices help users quickly spot trends ‚Äî such as whether students with longer screen time tend to sleep less, or whether higher coffee intake correlates with different sleep patterns. Rather than drawing strict conclusions, this chart encourages viewers to interpret the data themselves, supporting a more reflective and engaging storytelling experience.

The variables i used for this angles are

  • Screen time
  • Coffee consumption
  • Sleep duration
  • Gender
Angle 3: Scientific research on sleep duration for individuals between the ages of 18 and 40

This visualization communicates recommended sleep duration versus actual student sleep patterns, using a combination of a dot matrix and a donut chart. The donut chart on the right summarizes the same data proportionally, providing an at-a-glance overview of how many students fall into each category. Color coding differentiates the three categories clearly while maintaining accessibility, and the combination of detailed and summary views addresses both granular analysis and quick interpretation. Minimal text ensures the chart is readable and visually balanced, while a brief annotation guides understanding.
